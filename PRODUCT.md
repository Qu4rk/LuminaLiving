# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

High-income individuals considering an extended, exceptionally comfortable stay on the Limassol shoreline. They arrive at the site on their own time, often at night, weighing whether this residence is worth the 48-hour inquiry wait. Their context is low-stakes browsing with high-stakes intent: they have the money, they are deciding whether the place earns it. They are not shopping price; they are shopping taste. They leave if the site feels generic, salesy, or hollow.

## Product Purpose

A private coastal residence in Limassol, Cyprus, offered as an exclusive rental for stays from three days to a full year. The site's sole job is to make the right guest inquire, and to make the wrong guest self-select out. There is no booking flow, no calendar, no price list. Every inquiry is reviewed personally and answered within 48 hours with a tailored proposal. Success is a low volume of high-quality inquiries from guests who already understand what the residence is before they make contact.

## Brand Personality

Editorial, restrained, enthusiast-grade. The residence is built for genuine connoisseurs, not for visitors performing luxury. The coffee station is a real professional rig for actual brewing; the climate systems are real engineering; the interiors are composed, not staged. The voice is quiet, precise, and confident enough to understate everything. Three words: considered, precise, unhurried.

## Anti-references

- Stock AI designs. Generic templated layouts, identical card grids, hero-metric SaaS cliché, gradient text, decorative glassmorphism.
- Excessive emptiness. Big airy whitespace with nothing earned by it. Whitespace must be paid for with substance.
- Over-the-top animation. Bounce, elastic, parallax-for-parallax, breathing pulses, anything that performs motion instead of using it.
- Real-estate and booking clichés. Price sliders, availability calendars, stock photography of infinity pools, "book now" urgency.
- Spa-copy language. "Indulge", "unwind", "luxurious", "the ultimate". The systems are the luxury; the copy never says "luxury".

## Design Principles

1. Match the rest of the website. Redesigns never drift. A new block is only accepted if it reads as native to the existing system: same tokens, same type, same reveal language, same hairlines.
2. The systems are the luxury. Show real engineering (zoned climate, acoustic glazing, professional espresso rigs) as substance, not as copy. Never claim comfort; demonstrate the mechanism that produces it.
3. Expert confidence. Understate everything. If a feature needs a superlative to land, it is the wrong feature. Restraint is the signal of taste.
4. Whitespace must be paid for. Emptiness is permitted only when it carries rhythm. A block with one paragraph and a border is only acceptable if the rhythm demands a breath.
5. Motion serves reading. Animation is scroll-revealed, staggered, ease-out, and never performs. It exists to pace the reader, not to entertain.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- Reduced-motion respected. All scroll-revealed animations must degrade gracefully under `prefers-reduced-motion: reduce`; content remains fully visible without motion.
- Text contrast on tinted-black surfaces meets WCAG AA for body copy.
- No information conveyed by motion alone. Every animated reveal has a static fallback state.
